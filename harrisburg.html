Welcome to Harrisburg!

Nestled in Dauphin County in south-western Pennsylvania, Harrisburg is the state capitol. This urban city was incorporated in 1791 and has a population of 50,274 as of the year 2024. Compared to the rest of the state's average household income of $80,996, Harrisburg is below that number with $60,155 as the average.

With Harrisburg being the state capitol, it has great significance in its historical and cultural scene. This is evident from its beautiful architecture, such as the State Capitol Building, and the many events that take place throughout the year celebrating diversity and community. There is also plenty to do with attractions such as the Whitaker Center for Science and the Arts, as well as the Riverfront Park along the Susquehanna River. There is always local food and music to partake in while visiting this Pennsylvania centerpiece as well!

What Events Take Place In Harrisburg?

  • Pennsylvania Auto Show (late January to early February)
  • Great American Outdoor Show (world's largest outdoor recreation show) - February
  • Motorama (nation's largest all-indoor motorsports event) - February
  • Artsfest - late May
  • Ice & Fire Festival - March
