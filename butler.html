Welcome to Butler!

Butler is lesser-known for being the birthplace of the Jeep, and a hidden gem in the Greater Pittsburgh Area to live in and visit. Butler is located in Butler County in southwestern Pennsylvania. This suburban city was incorporated in 1918 and has a population of 12,873 as of the year 2024. Compared to the rest of the state's average household income of $80,996, Butler is below that number with $56,690 as the average.

In the city of Butler, you can see plenty of the small-town history that makes this town a spot on the map worth visiting! Its residents celebrate being the birthplace of the Jeep by putting on the Bantam Jeep Heritage Festival each year and showcasing various makes and models of Jeeps. Fun fact - Jeep owners often leave rubber ducks on Jeeps they see to show that another Jeep owner liked their ride. What a creative way to show community! Other community-driven events include the Butler Fall Festival and the Italian Festival, which offers incredible opportunities to experience the warmth and hospitality of this little town. Main Street in Butler is filled with many little Mom-and-Pop shops, where you can find everything from fresh-ground coffee to antiques to clothing and everything in between! Come visit us today!

What Events Take Place In Butler?

  • Butler County Fair - July
  • Butler Fall Festival - early October
  • Jeep Festival - June
  • Butler Italian Festival - June
  • Christmas Parade - late November or early December
